01 Approach

The economics of an AI SaaS business are not the economics of a traditional SaaS business.

Inference cost flows through cost of goods sold, compressing gross margin to ranges (50–70 percent) that conventional SaaS benchmarks misread as alarming. Usage-based pricing breaks the standard lifetime-value formula. Cohort retention curves behave differently when the product itself is improving with each model release. None of this fits the templates a generalist fractional CFO will hand you.

Most existing finance advisors apply 2018-era SaaS frameworks to 2026-vintage AI companies. The conclusions that result are confident and frequently wrong. Founders walk into investor meetings with numbers that misrepresent their own business, and lose rounds they should have won.

We work narrowly so we can work deeply. The practice does one thing: help AI SaaS founders understand their real economics, in language that holds up under sceptical questioning.

  • i.

    Inference‑adjusted gross margin

    True unit cost modelling, by tier and by user behaviour, not flat assumptions imported from horizontal SaaS.

  • ii.

    Cohort retention truth

    Pulling raw subscription data and rebuilding cohorts from the ground up. The first time most founders see how their business actually retains.

  • iii.

    Investor‑defensible documentation

    Models and reports calibrated to survive due diligence from a fund that has seen two hundred AI decks this quarter.
